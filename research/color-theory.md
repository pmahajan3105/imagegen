# Sci/Art 12-Season Color Theory Reference

## Sourcing and methodology

This document is an independent compilation of Christine Scaman's **Sci/Art 12-Season Color Theory** as published at **12blueprints.com** and corroborated by Sci/Art-trained analysts. It is grounded in the Munsell color system (hue, value, chroma), which is the dimensional framework Scaman teaches.

**Primary source.** 12blueprints.com (Christine Scaman): blog posts, season descriptions, eyeshadow/neutrals product listings, and the e-book *Return to Your Natural Colours*.

**Secondary sources (Sci/Art-aligned analysts).** Gabrielle Arruda (gabriellearruda.com, explicitly Sci/Art trained), Chrysalis Colour (chrysaliscolour.com), Elemental Colour (elementalcolour.com.au, Sarah Tearle), The Concept Wardrobe (theconceptwardrobe.com), and PalettePath (palettepath.com).

**Excluded.** House of Colour, Color Me Beautiful, Korean personal color systems, and other non-Sci/Art frameworks.

**Hex code approach.** Scaman, Sarah Tearle, and licensed Sci/Art analysts publish swatches as physical fans and posters (TCI, NDU, 12 Blueprints fabric sets) and do not publish authoritative RGB values. The hex codes here are best-fit estimates derived from (1) named-swatch descriptions in 12blueprints product listings and blog posts, (2) Munsell-aligned hue/value/chroma ranges per season, and (3) cross-referenced color-name conventions across the secondary sources. **Treat them as representative, not absolute.** Confidence on traits and rules is **High**; confidence on hex precision is **Medium to Medium-High** per season. For exact production matching, the physical 12 Blueprints / TCI / NDU fans remain authoritative.

**Format.** Hex values are 6-digit uppercase `#RRGGBB`. IDs are kebab-case. Seasons are presented in canonical order: Spring (Light, True, Bright), Summer (Light, True, Soft), Autumn (Soft, True, Dark), Winter (True, Bright, Dark).

# Spring family

All Springs share a warm (yellow-based) undertone and a high-light-energy quality. They reject black and pure white at the face. Metals lean yellow gold over silver.

## Light Spring

- **ID:** `light-spring`
- **Family:** spring
- **Aliases:** Light Spring (canonical). Sister to Light Summer and True Spring.

**Traits**
- Undertone: **neutral-warm** (warm dominant with a Summer-cool whisper)
- Depth: **light** (highest value of any Spring; the primary characteristic)
- Chroma: **clear** (medium-high; reads pastel because lightness plus brightness equals pastel)
- Contrast: **low** (Scaman: extremes of light and dark are absent)

Munsell positioning: high value (8–9), moderate chroma (4–8), warm hue band 5YR–10Y leaning toward 2.5Y.

**Best Neutrals**
- Vanilla Cream / Warm Ivory `#F7EBD4`
- Eggshell Off-White `#F4ECDC`
- Warm Stone Gray (Light Spring's "black") `#8C8475`
- Light Warm Taupe / Linen `#C9B79A`
- Light Camel / Hazelnut `#C8A67E`
- Soft Dove Gray `#B6B0A0`
- Light Warm Denim / Chambray `#8FA7BC`

**Signature Colors**
- Peach `#F4B999`
- Light Coral / Salmon `#F69E8E`
- Warm Pastel Pink `#F7B6BC`
- Buttercup / Light Daffodil `#F8DC75`
- Light Mint Green `#B6E3B8`
- Light Aqua / Spring Turquoise `#7CCFC8`
- Soft Sky Blue `#9CC8E0`
- Cantaloupe / Light Apricot `#F6B97B`

**Accent Colors**
- Hot Coral `#F37C5E`
- Light Periwinkle `#A6A8DC`
- Clear Pastel Lime `#CDE17A`
- Tea Rose `#EDA0A8`
- Light Chinese Blue (darkness limit) `#5E8AAE`

**Use Carefully**
- True Black `#000000` (cold and dark; replace with warm stone gray)
- Pure White `#FFFFFF` (too cool/harsh; use vanilla cream)
- Charcoal `#36454F` (exceeds darkness limit)
- Burnt Orange `#CC5500` (too saturated)
- Deep Navy `#1F2A66` (too dark for low-contrast face)
- Hot Magenta `#E0218A` (reads Bright Winter or Bright Spring)

**Rules**
- Keep value range light to light-medium; never exceed warm granite or stone gray near the face.
- Replace true white with vanilla cream and black with warm stone gray.
- Wear at least one colorful piece per outfit; all-neutral looks drain vitality.
- Choose warm light gold or polished light metals; avoid antiqued or oxidized finishes.
- Pair analogous hues at similar value to mirror the natural low contrast.
- Avoid icy, glittery, or stark Winter effects; the Light Spring effect is creamy and dewy, not frosted.

## True Spring

- **ID:** `true-spring`
- **Family:** spring
- **Aliases:** Warm Spring, Golden Spring. Original Spring of the 4-season system.

**Traits**
- Undertone: **warm** (pure warm, no blue undertones; lighter and brighter than Autumn's gold)
- Depth: **medium** (range from buttery ivory to warm dove gray; no extremely dark colors)
- Chroma: **bright / clear** (high chroma; saturated and vivid)
- Contrast: **medium** (visible feature contrast without true white or black extremes)

Munsell positioning: hue 5YR–7.5Y (most yellow-orange seasonal palette), value 6–8, chroma 8–14.

**Best Neutrals**
- Warm Ivory / Buttermilk `#F6EBC9`
- French Vanilla / Almond Cream `#F2E2BD`
- Light Yolk / Cornbread Beige `#EBD18A`
- Warm Camel / Honey Tan `#C99A5B`
- Golden Khaki `#B5A06A`
- Warm Chocolate Brown `#6F4A2D`
- Hippo Gray-Brown (True Spring's "black") `#6E6657`

**Signature Colors**
- Clear Coral `#F37855`
- Peach / Apricot `#F8A572`
- Warm Bright Yellow / Daffodil `#F5C84B`
- Mandarin Orange `#F08A3E`
- Tomato Red `#E04E2A`
- Warm Grass Green / Daiquiri `#7BBB4F`
- Clear Turquoise / Lagoon `#3FB6B8`
- Warm Pastel Aqua / Mint `#9DD9C5`

**Accent Colors**
- Hot Watermelon Pink `#F25979`
- Periwinkle / Pansy `#7B7CCB`
- Bright Lime `#B7D24A`
- Sugar Syrup / Warm Salmon Pink `#F49F92`
- Marina / Cobalt Spring Blue `#3066B0`

**Use Carefully**
- True Black `#000000` (too cold/dark; replace with hippo gray-brown or warm chocolate)
- True White `#FFFFFF` (too cool/harsh; use cream or buttermilk)
- Icy Pastel `#D6E9F5` (clashes with pure warmth)
- Dusty Rust `#B7410E` (Autumn shade dampens brightness)
- Burgundy / Wine `#722F37` (too cool and dark)
- Cool Fuchsia `#D81B60` (a pure-warm palette cannot host blue-pink)

**Rules**
- Eliminate black and pure white from the face; substitute warm ivory and warm chocolate.
- Lead with warmth in every piece; if it leans cool, drop it.
- Wear at least one bright or clear color per outfit; never head-to-toe neutrals.
- Choose light, shiny yellow gold over silver; rose gold works only if peachy.
- Pair value contrast with hue contrast (teal with peach, coral with turquoise).
- Prefer dewy, glossy, juicy finishes; avoid heavy matte or dusty fabrics.

## Bright Spring

- **ID:** `bright-spring`
- **Family:** spring
- **Aliases:** Clear Spring. Spring–Winter neutral season; sister to Bright Winter.

**Traits**
- Undertone: **neutral-warm** (warm dominant with Winter cool influence)
- Depth: **medium** (range from light warm beige to warmed charcoal; mid-value cluster)
- Chroma: **bright** (very high; only Bright Winter rivals it)
- Contrast: **medium-high to high** (Scaman: wider light to dark than other Springs)

Munsell positioning: hue 5YR–5Y leaning warm, value 5–7, chroma 10–16.

**Signature Colors**
- Bright Coral `#FF6F4D`
- Hot Pink / Watermelon `#F73E76`
- Lime / Apple Green `#9CCB3B`
- Bright Turquoise `#1FBFC5`
- Bright Royal/Cobalt Blue `#1F4FC8`
- Bright Lemon-Warm Yellow `#F5D324`
- Tropical Emerald `#1FB36A`
- Warm Fuchsia `#D8328C`

**Accent Colors**
- Persimmon `#F26B1F`
- Pansy Purple `#7C3FB4`
- Electric Aqua `#3FD8D5`
- Tomato Red `#E63B2A`
- Bright Buttercream `#F8E36A`

**Use Carefully**
- Pure Black `#000000` (usable but never solo at the face; mix with bright color)
- Stark True White `#FFFFFF` (too icy; use warm off-white)
- Mustard `#C9A227` (Autumn earthiness dulls brightness)

- Cool Icy Pastel `#D9E9F5` (clashes with warm undertone)
- Burnt Camel-Orange `#A0522D` (Autumn richness reads heavy)

**Rules**
- Wear at least one fully saturated palette color near the face; muted-only outfits flatten Bright Spring.
- Use hue contrast (coral with turquoise, lime with magenta) over value contrast alone.
- Pair black with a bright accent; never wear black solo at the face.
- Choose shiny, polished finishes in metals and fabrics; avoid matte and antiqued.
- Replace burnt orange and earthy browns with warm chocolate or warm camel.
- Embrace clear, glassy effects (icier lights and high contrast as Winter influence arrives).

# Summer family

All Summers share a cool (blue-based) undertone and tend toward soft and muted compared to Winters. They reject pure black and pure white at the face. Metals lean silver, platinum, and white gold.

## Light Summer

- **ID:** `light-summer`
- **Family:** summer
- **Aliases:** Light Summer, Cool Light, Sunlit Summer. Sister to Light Spring and True Summer.

**Traits**
- Undertone: **neutral-cool** (cool with a small Spring-derived warmth dab)
- Depth: **light**
- Chroma: **soft** (medium; the brightest of the Summers, sometimes called "soft-bright")
- Contrast: **low**

**Best Neutrals**
- Vanilla Ice Cream White `#F4ECDD`
- Light Pink Beige (Peige) `#E6D5CC`
- Dove Gray `#C9CDD0`
- Cool Light Taupe `#ACA6A2`

- Soft Charcoal Navy `#475467`

**Signature Colors**
- Powder Blue `#B0D4E3`
- Cornflower Blue `#7FB3D5`
- Periwinkle `#A6B6E0`
- Lavender `#C9B8E0`
- Light Wisteria `#B7A4D1`
- Cool Watermelon Pink `#F0A6BB`
- Sea Mist Mint `#B7DEC9`
- Light Rose `#E8B7C2`

**Accent Colors**
- Sky Blue `#A8C8E5`
- Light Orchid `#DBBAD9`
- Cool Coral Pink `#F2A6A0`
- Aster Purple `#8E7FB6`
- Cool Mint / Sea Green `#9DCFB6`

**Use Carefully**
- Cool Raspberry (deepest red) `#B85D7A`
- Spruce Green (darkness limit) `#3F6B5C`
- Cadet Blue `#5F8C95`
- Ash Brown (darkness limit) `#6E5E58`
- Soft Black / Deep Koala Gray `#3E3F46`
- Cool Light Lemon (use sparingly) `#F1EAB6`

**Rules**
- Keep value light overall; never go deeper than soft charcoal or rose-brown near the face.
- Stay neutral-cool; lead with blues but allow a vanilla or pink-gold whisper of Spring warmth.
- Replace pure white with vanilla and pure black with soft koala gray or charcoal navy.
- Use silver and white-gold metals in matte or satin finishes, not high-gloss.
- Build low-contrast outfits with pastels and light-medium neutrals; avoid black-and-white pairings.
- Use clear cool pastels as accents to repeat eye color and lift the skin.

# Autumn family

All Autumns share a warm (yellow/golden-based) undertone with muted to deep saturation. They reject black and pure white at the face. Metals lean gold, copper, brass, and bronze.

## Soft Autumn

- **ID:** `soft-autumn`
- **Family:** autumn
- **Aliases:** Muted Autumn, Tinted Autumn, Soft Warm. Sister to Soft Summer.

**Traits**
- Undertone: **neutral-warm** (warm-neutral; both gold and silver work, gold preferred)
- Depth: **medium** (does not reach extremes of light or dark)
- Chroma: **muted** (lowest chroma of the Autumns; primary aspect)
- Contrast: **low** (low to low-medium)

Skin texture metaphor (Scaman): "suede."

**Best Neutrals**
- Eggshell / Rice Cake `#F1E7D2`
- Oat Beige / Sand `#D7C4A3`
- Mushroom Taupe `#A89684`
- Warm Wood-Ash Gray `#8C8275`
- Walnut Brown `#7A5E48`
- Sepia / Cocoa Brown (Soft Autumn "black") `#5A4232`
- Dusty Warm Charcoal `#4B423A`

**Signature Colors**
- Golden Apricot `#E0B080`
- Muskmelon / Warm Peach `#D89978`
- Coral Rose `#C58775`
- Dusty Rose `#B98A85`
- Fern / Soft Olive Green `#8A8A5C`
- Sage Green `#A6B08C`
- Weathered Turquoise / Soft Teal `#5E8A88`
- Soft Mustard / Weathered Ochre `#C8A364`

**Accent Colors**
- Spiced Apple / Soft Brick `#B5604A`
- Dusty Periwinkle (warm-leaning) `#7A8AA8`
- Muted Plum `#7E5A6E`
- Light Aloe / Willow Green `#9DAE82`
- Toasted Camel `#B89368`

**Use Carefully**
- True Black `#000000` (too dark; ages and boxes the appearance)
- Pure White `#FFFFFF` (too cool and harsh)
- Bright Cool Fuchsia `#D6206E` (cool clash; clownish)
- Saturated Orange `#FF6A00` (overpowers; reads artificial)
- Royal Blue / Sapphire `#1F3A93` (dominates the wearer)
- Cool Charcoal `#2A2A2A` (drains the face; use cocoa or sepia)

**Rules**
- Aim for medium-light to medium-dark overall value; avoid value extremes.
- Combine monochromatically or with neighboring hues at similar value; avoid hue-opposites and high contrast.
- Replace black with cocoa, sepia, or dusty warm charcoal; replace white with eggshell or oat.
- Choose brushed, matte, antiqued, or hammered metals; soft yellow gold, rose gold, antiqued silver, pewter all work.
- Keep prints small, loose, organic (leaves, ovals, tiny florals); avoid stark geometric or high-contrast prints.
- In makeup, stay in muted browns and taupes; no true black mascara or liner.

# Winter family

All Winters share a cool (blue-based) undertone with high saturation and high contrast capacity. Metals lean silver, platinum, and white gold; yellow gold is generally avoided.

# Methodology caveats

1. **Hex codes are derived, not sampled.** No Sci/Art analyst publishes authoritative RGB values. Hex above is best-fit estimation from named-swatch descriptions and Munsell-aligned ranges. Treat as directional.
2. **Trait and rule confidence is High.** Cross-source agreement on undertone, depth, chroma, contrast, and the sister-season topology is strong across 12blueprints, Chrysalis Colour, Concept Wardrobe, Elemental Colour, Gabrielle Arruda, and PalettePath.
3. **Minor source disagreements exist.** Arruda sometimes labels chroma a half-step lower than Scaman canon (e.g., True Spring "medium-high" instead of "high"). This document follows Sci/Art canon when sources conflict.
4. **For exact production matching**, source the physical 12 Blueprints fabric/neutrals sets, TCI fans, or NDU drapes. Digital sRGB cannot fully reproduce Munsell pigment swatches.
