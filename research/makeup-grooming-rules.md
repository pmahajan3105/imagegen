This file uses makeup-artist color theory, professional shade-matching logic, dermatology-aware tinted sunscreen guidance, and grooming sources to translate undertone, depth, chroma, and contrast into brand-agnostic shade-direction rules. Actual products, swatches, and shade names should be selected by the app from the user's locked palette and presentation settings.

# Part 1: Makeup Direction (feminine / feminine-leaning)

## Foundation & Complexion

**ID:** `foundation-complexion`
**Goal:** Match skin without over-correcting; finish keyed to chroma.

### Safe everyday
- **Warm × light:** ivory-to-warm-beige range; golden or peach base; sheer-to-medium skin-like coverage.
- **Warm × medium:** warm-beige to honey-tan range; golden, peach, or olive-gold base; medium coverage.
- **Warm × deep:** warm-umber to espresso range; golden-brown, red-brown, or warm mahogany base.
- **Cool × light:** porcelain-to-cool-beige range; pink, rose, or blue-pink base; sheer-to-medium coverage.
- **Cool × medium:** rose-beige to cool-tan range; pink-neutral or red-beige base; medium coverage.
- **Cool × deep:** cool-cocoa to cool-espresso range; red, red-violet, or blue-red base.
- **Neutral × light:** neutral ivory-to-light-beige range; balanced beige base; borrow warm for sallowness, cool for surface redness.
- **Neutral × medium:** neutral sand-to-caramel range; balanced beige, olive-beige, or soft peach-beige base.
- **Neutral × deep:** neutral cocoa-to-espresso range; balanced red-gold base; choose by neck/chest and oxidation behavior.

### Statement
- **Warm × any depth:** same undertone match, upgraded with dewy warmth or polished golden-satin dimension.
- **Cool × any depth:** same undertone match, upgraded with rose-satin luminosity; keep the base refined, not flat.
- **Neutral × any depth:** balanced satin finish; lean warm or cool only through removable accents.

### Finish guidance
- Soft chroma → satin or soft-matte finish that blurs rather than shines.
- Clear / bright chroma → dewy, luminous, or polished satin finish.
- Low contrast → sheer-to-medium coverage; high contrast → more perfected coverage can read intentional.

### Use carefully
- Undertone over-correction: yellow on cool skin, pink on warm skin, or gray cast on neutral/deep skin.
- Heavy full-coverage base when the shade is not exact; mismatch reads louder than texture.
- Pale sunscreen or base pigments that leave a white, blue, or gray cast on medium/deep skin.

## Concealer

**ID:** `concealer`
**Goal:** Brighten under-eye and even tone without depositing chalk.

### Safe everyday
- **Warm × light:** warm ivory-to-light beige; peach-brightening corrector only where blue/purple shows.
- **Warm × medium:** warm beige-to-honey; peach-apricot corrector for under-eye or spots.
- **Warm × deep:** warm tan-to-deep brown; orange, red-orange, or warm caramel correction under a matching concealer.
- **Cool × light:** cool porcelain-to-rose beige; pink-peach brightener used sparingly.
- **Cool × medium:** cool beige-to-rose tan; muted peach or rose corrector with neutral-cool concealer.
- **Cool × deep:** cool cocoa-to-espresso; red-orange or berry-brown correction under a cool deep match.
- **Neutral × light:** neutral ivory-to-light beige; choose peach for blue shadows, pink-beige for dullness.
- **Neutral × medium:** neutral beige-to-caramel; peach, apricot, or neutral-caramel correction by discoloration.
- **Neutral × deep:** neutral cocoa-to-espresso; orange/red-orange correction softened with balanced deep concealer.

### Statement
- **Warm × any depth:** sculpted brightening one value step lighter, not a white highlight.
- **Cool × any depth:** rose-neutral brightening that lifts without going icy.
- **Neutral × any depth:** balanced brightening; shift peach or rose based on the discoloration, not the trend.

### Finish guidance
- Soft chroma → satin concealer set lightly so the under-eye remains diffused.
- Clear / bright chroma → skin-like satin with targeted luminosity on inner corners only.
- High contrast can carry more defined brightening; low contrast needs subtle value shifts.

### Use carefully
- Concealer two or more levels lighter than skin depth; it creates gray, chalky, or flashback effects.
- Heavy triangle placement when the goal is natural correction.
- Corrector left unblended or unlayered; correction should disappear into the complexion.

## Blush

**ID:** `blush`
**Goal:** Add warmth or cool flush; placement varies by face shape and the app's face-shape rules.

### Safe everyday
- **Warm × light:** soft peach, apricot, warm pink, or peach-beige flush.
- **Warm × medium:** warm rose, coral, terracotta-pink, or muted cinnamon.
- **Warm × deep:** brick-rose, burnt coral, warm berry, raisin-terracotta, or red-brown flush.
- **Cool × light:** cool pink, soft rose, mauve-pink, or delicate berry stain.
- **Cool × medium:** rose-plum, berry-mauve, cool raspberry, or muted wine.
- **Cool × deep:** deep berry, plum, wine-rose, blue-red berry, or cool raisin.
- **Neutral × light:** soft rose, peach-rose, neutral pink, or gentle apricot-rose.
- **Neutral × medium:** rosewood, neutral mauve, soft coral-rose, or balanced berry.
- **Neutral × deep:** berry-brown, deep rosewood, neutral plum, or red-brown rose.

### Statement
- **Warm × light/medium:** clear coral, vivid peach, poppy, or warm watermelon used in sheer layers.
- **Warm × deep:** brick red, burnt orange, deep coral, or warm currant with enough pigment payoff.
- **Cool × light/medium:** raspberry, cool rose, clear pink, or blue-red berry diffused at the edge.
- **Cool × deep:** wine, plum, blackberry, or saturated berry layered thinly.
- **Neutral × light/medium:** rose-coral, clear rose, balanced pink, or soft berry.
- **Neutral × deep:** deep rosewood, cranberry, berry-brown, or balanced plum.

### Finish guidance
- Soft chroma → powder, cream-powder, or satin cream with diffused edges.
- Clear / bright chroma → fresh cream, gel, or luminous satin can look alive.
- High contrast can hold stronger cheek color; low contrast needs watercolor placement.

### Use carefully
- Very pale powder blush on deep skin; it can read as ash instead of flush.
- Very blue pink on warm skin or very orange coral on cool skin unless intentionally editorial.
- Frosted blush across texture; keep shimmer strategic and placed high.

## Eyeshadow

**ID:** `eyeshadow`
**Goal:** Define and complement eye color; harmonize with locked-palette accent colors.

### Safe everyday
- **Warm × light:** warm beige, sand, soft camel, peach-taupe, or light bronze.
- **Warm × medium:** camel, warm taupe, bronze, olive-brown, or soft terracotta.
- **Warm × deep:** deep bronze, cocoa, espresso-brown, olive-blackened brown, or warm plum-brown.
- **Cool × light:** cool taupe, soft gray, rose-beige, mauve-taupe, or misty plum.
- **Cool × medium:** slate taupe, mauve-brown, cool gray-brown, pewter, or muted plum.
- **Cool × deep:** charcoal, espresso-plum, gunmetal, deep mauve-brown, or blackened berry.
- **Neutral × light:** mushroom, soft taupe, beige-rose, muted bronze-taupe, or light greige.
- **Neutral × medium:** taupe-brown, rose-brown, bronze-pewter, muted olive, or balanced cocoa.
- **Neutral × deep:** deep taupe, espresso, plum-brown, bronze-smoke, or neutral charcoal.

### Statement
- **Warm × light/medium:** copper, golden bronze, clear olive, warm terracotta, or amber shimmer.
- **Warm × deep:** molten bronze, deep copper, burnt sienna, blackened olive, or warm jewel tones.
- **Cool × light/medium:** pewter, lavender-gray, cool rose metallic, slate, or soft sapphire wash.
- **Cool × deep:** gunmetal, eggplant, deep teal, sapphire, blackberry, or cool metallic smoke.
- **Neutral × light/medium:** mixed-metal taupe, rose-bronze, champagne-taupe, or balanced smoky brown.
- **Neutral × deep:** bronze-pewter, plum-brown, deep neutral smoke, or muted jewel tones.

### Glasses / frame interaction
- Heavy or dark frames → lighter lid shade, stronger lash definition, and clean brows.
- Thin, rimless, or low-contrast frames → more visible lid color or a soft statement liner can work.
- Strong prescriptions change perceived eye size; scale liner and mascara after lens effect, not before.

### Finish guidance
- Soft chroma → matte, satin, or low-metallic shimmer with blended edges.
- Clear / bright chroma → polished shimmer, clean metallic, or sharper contrast placement.
- High contrast can carry smoky depth; low contrast benefits from gradient definition.

### Use carefully
- Ashy gray on warm undertones or orange-brown on cool undertones unless softened with a bridge shade.
- Glitter fallout near mature, dry, or sensitive eyes.
- Statement eye plus statement lip; choose one dominant focal point unless the occasion is deliberately editorial.

## Eyeliner & Mascara

**ID:** `eyeliner-mascara`
**Goal:** Define lash and eye line; intensity keyed to contrast.

### Safe everyday
- **Warm × light:** soft brown, warm taupe, bronze-brown, or brown mascara.
- **Warm × medium:** espresso, warm brown, bronze-black, or deep brown mascara.
- **Warm × deep:** deep espresso, soft black-brown, warm plum-black, or blackened bronze.
- **Cool × light:** cool brown, taupe-gray, soft charcoal, or brown-black mascara.
- **Cool × medium:** cool espresso, charcoal, black-brown, or muted plum-black.
- **Cool × deep:** soft black, cool black-brown, charcoal-black, or blackened berry.
- **Neutral × light:** neutral taupe, soft brown-black, or balanced brown mascara.
- **Neutral × medium:** neutral espresso, charcoal-brown, or brown-black.
- **Neutral × deep:** neutral black-brown, soft black, or espresso-black.

### Statement
- **Warm × any depth:** bronze, copper-brown, deep olive, or warm plum liner; scale darkness to contrast.
- **Cool × any depth:** charcoal, navy-black, plum, or cool graphite liner; keep edges clean.
- **Neutral × any depth:** espresso, black-brown, graphite, or muted jewel liner chosen by outfit accent.

### Glasses / frame interaction
- Match liner weight to frame weight: heavier frames can hold a stronger lash line; fine frames need restraint.
- Nearsighted lens effect can shrink the eye; keep lower liner soft and open the inner rim if desired.
- Farsighted lens effect magnifies texture; use separated lashes and tidy liner.

### Finish guidance
- Soft chroma → matte or satin liner; smudge softly at the lash line.
- Clear / bright chroma → crisp gel, liquid, or polished pencil can read clean.
- High contrast can use black; low contrast should start with brown, gray-brown, or espresso.

### Use carefully
- Jet-black liner around the full eye on low-contrast or light-depth faces.
- Heavy lower waterline without upper-lash balance.
- Colored mascara when the lip or blush is already the statement.

## Brow

**ID:** `brow`
**Goal:** Frame the face; tone keyed to hair color.

### Safe everyday
- **Warm × light:** soft taupe-blonde, warm blonde, or light warm brown; keep edges feathery.
- **Warm × medium:** warm brown, chestnut-brown, or soft brunette; match depth without orange cast.
- **Warm × deep:** deep brown, warm espresso, or soft black-brown; keep hair strokes visible.
- **Cool × light:** ash blonde, ash taupe, or soft cool beige-brown.
- **Cool × medium:** ash brown, cool brunette, mushroom brown, or soft charcoal-brown.
- **Cool × deep:** cool espresso, soft black, or black-brown; not flat marker black.
- **Neutral × light:** neutral taupe, soft blonde-brown, or beige-brown; choose hair-root match first.
- **Neutral × medium:** neutral brown, soft brunette, or taupe-brown; adjust warm/cool to hair cast.
- **Neutral × deep:** neutral espresso, soft black-brown, or deep taupe-brown.

### Statement
- **Warm × any depth:** slightly fuller warm-brown definition; pair with clean skin and softer eye.
- **Cool × any depth:** sharper ash/espresso structure; keep fill hair-like.
- **Neutral × any depth:** balanced brow structure; intensity follows contrast, not trend.

### Hair-color cross-reference
- Blonde → one to two levels deeper than hair, unless brows are naturally darker.
- Brunette → match hair depth or go one level softer for a less severe frame.
- Gray / silver → soft taupe, ash brown, or gray-brown; skip yellow-beige.
- Red / copper → auburn-tinted brown, warm taupe, or soft chestnut; do not over-orange.
- Black → soft black, black-brown, or cool espresso; not flat jet black.
- Vivid dyed hair → anchor brows to natural root or a neutralized version of the hair family.

### Finish guidance
- Soft chroma → powder, pencil, or tinted gel with blurred density.
- Clear / bright chroma → cleaner edges and stronger grooming can look intentional.
- High contrast can hold a fuller brow; low contrast needs softer pressure.

### Use carefully
- Brow product that exactly matches a vivid hair dye; it can costume the face.
- Block brows with solid fronts; keep the inner brow lighter or brushed through.
- Warm brow tones on cool-gray hair or black fill on fine/light features.

## Lip

**ID:** `lip`
**Goal:** Anchor or accent the look; intensity keyed to contrast and presence of other statement makeup.

### Safe everyday
- **Warm × light:** peach nude, warm pink, apricot balm, or soft coral-beige.
- **Warm × medium:** warm rose, caramel-rose, muted coral, terracotta-pink, or spiced nude.
- **Warm × deep:** warm rose-brown, terracotta, brick-rose, cocoa-rose, or burnt caramel.
- **Cool × light:** soft rose, mauve-pink, cool pink-beige, or light berry tint.
- **Cool × medium:** rosewood, mauve, berry-rose, plum-pink, or cool brown-rose.
- **Cool × deep:** berry, plum, wine, cool cocoa-rose, or blue-red stain.
- **Neutral × light:** peach-rose, pink-beige, soft rose, or balanced nude.
- **Neutral × medium:** neutral rose, rose-brown, balanced mauve, or muted berry.
- **Neutral × deep:** deep rosewood, berry-brown, plum-brown, or balanced wine.

### Statement
- **Warm × light/medium:** clear coral, tomato red, warm poppy, or orange-red.
- **Warm × deep:** brick red, burnt cherry, deep terracotta, warm wine, or red-brown.
- **Cool × light/medium:** blue-red, raspberry, fuchsia-rose, cherry, or cool berry.
- **Cool × deep:** blackberry, burgundy, deep plum, blue-red wine, or aubergine.
- **Neutral × light/medium:** balanced red, rose-red, clear berry, or pink-coral.
- **Neutral × deep:** cranberry, deep rosewood, balanced burgundy, or plum-red.

### Finish guidance
- Soft chroma → satin, balm, blurred matte, or stain; keep edges diffused.
- Clear / bright chroma → polished satin, lacquered balm, clean matte, or glassy sheer.
- High contrast can carry opaque color; low contrast should sheer or blur statement shades.

### Use carefully
- Pale beige lips below the user's skin depth; they can erase the mouth.
- Blue-heavy lip on warm undertones or orange-heavy lip on cool undertones without a bridge cheek.
- Statement lip with statement eye; one focal point leads, the other supports unless deliberately editorial.

# Part 2: Grooming Direction (masculine / masculine-leaning)

## Skin Tonics & Sunscreen Tints

**ID:** `skin-tonics`
**Goal:** Even tone, healthy finish, sunscreen with skin-tone tints.

### Direction by undertone
- **Warm:** golden-beige, peach-beige, amber-beige, or warm-brown tints that reduce gray cast.
- **Cool:** rose-beige, red-brown, cool cocoa, or muted pink-neutral tints that do not turn yellow.
- **Neutral:** balanced beige, olive-beige, neutral tan, or neutral brown tints selected by skin depth.

### Use carefully
- Universal tint on very light, olive, or deep skin when it turns orange, pink, gray, or chalky.
- High-glow finishes in conservative work settings; keep finish healthy, not shiny.
- Tinted sunscreen used too sparingly; protection still depends on enough product and reapplication.

### Notes
- Choose tint by both skin depth and undertone; the best result should disappear at jaw, neck, and beard line.

## Lip Balm Tones

**ID:** `lip-balm`
**Goal:** Tinted balm direction (no full lipstick); subtle warmth or cool.

### Direction by undertone
- **Warm:** clear, golden-peach, warm-coral, amber, or soft caramel tint; skin-toned plus warmth.
- **Cool:** clear, rose, mauve-rose, plum-tinted, or cool berry balm; soft pigment only.
- **Neutral:** clear, pink-beige, rose-brown, or skin-toned balm; "your lips but steadier."

### Use carefully
- Full lipstick payoff; that belongs in the Makeup Direction variant.
- Frost, glitter, or high-shine gloss outside event styling.
- Tint that is lighter than natural lip depth; it can read chalky.

### Notes
- Balm should correct dryness and add quiet tone, not announce itself as a lip color.

## Beard Care Tones

**ID:** `beard-care`
**Goal:** Beard oil, beard balm, dye direction (if applicable).

### Direction by undertone × depth
- **Warm × light:** clear oils or warm-blonde/light-brown tint; beige-gold rather than orange.
- **Warm × medium:** warm brown, soft chestnut, or medium brunette tint; keep gray blending translucent.
- **Warm × deep:** deep brown, warm espresso, or soft mahogany-brown; not opaque black.
- **Cool × light:** clear oils or ash-blonde/light-taupe tint; reduce yellowing.
- **Cool × medium:** ash brown, cool brunette, or soft mushroom-brown tint.
- **Cool × deep:** cool espresso, soft black-brown, or charcoal-brown; maintain natural variation.
- **Neutral × light:** clear oils, neutral blonde, or beige-brown tint; choose by scalp hair root.
- **Neutral × medium:** neutral brown or soft brunette tint; adjust warmth by beard's natural cast.
- **Neutral × deep:** neutral espresso, deep brown, or soft black-brown; keep edges natural.

### Use carefully
- Dye darker than the natural beard or scalp reference; it can look artificial quickly.
- Solid dye on sparse beards; tinting colors existing hair but does not create density.
- Chemical dye without a patch test, especially around the mouth, neck, or sensitive skin.

### Notes
- For gray blending, choose one level lighter than the natural beard and comb through for dimension.

## Eyebrow Direction

**ID:** `eyebrow-direction`
**Goal:** Brow grooming, fill (if applicable), tone keyed to hair color.

### Direction by undertone × depth
- **Warm × light:** clear gel, warm taupe, or light warm-brown fill; use minimal density.
- **Warm × medium:** warm brown, chestnut-brown, or soft brunette fill; follow natural brow shape.
- **Warm × deep:** deep brown or warm espresso fill; brush through to prevent blockiness.
- **Cool × light:** clear gel, ash taupe, ash blonde, or cool beige-brown fill.
- **Cool × medium:** ash brown, mushroom brown, or cool brunette fill; keep lines hair-like.
- **Cool × deep:** cool espresso, charcoal-brown, or soft black-brown fill.
- **Neutral × light:** clear gel, neutral taupe, or blonde-brown fill matched to hair root.
- **Neutral × medium:** neutral brown or taupe-brown fill; choose soft definition over heavy shape.
- **Neutral × deep:** neutral espresso, soft black-brown, or deep taupe-brown fill.

### Hair-color cross-reference
- Blonde → one to two levels deeper than hair; keep undertone beige or taupe.
- Brunette → match hair depth or go one level softer for natural framing.
- Gray / silver → soft taupe, ash brown, or gray-brown; use yellow-beige only when hair is genuinely warm.
- Red / copper → auburn-brown, warm taupe, or soft chestnut; keep it muted.
- Black → soft black, black-brown, or cool espresso; not flat jet black.
- Vivid dyed hair → use natural root or neutralized hair-family tone.

### Use carefully
- Over-tweezing or shaving brow hair; grooming should clean, not redesign.
- Hard squared fronts or filled skin with no visible hair strokes.
- Brow color much warmer or darker than hair unless the look is intentionally fashion-forward.

### Notes
- Groom first, fill second; most masculine-leaning polish comes from trimming and brushing before pigment.

## Complexion Enhancers

**ID:** `complexion-enhancers`
**Goal:** Light bronzer, healthy flush, tinted moisturizer — subtle, not glam.

### Direction by undertone × depth
- **Warm × light:** sheer warm beige tint, peach-beige warmth, or soft golden bronzing at the edge.
- **Warm × medium:** honey, caramel, amber, or soft terracotta warmth; keep skin texture visible.
- **Warm × deep:** warm cocoa, red-brown, mahogany, or deep amber warmth with no ash.
- **Cool × light:** rose-beige tint, cool beige, or very soft taupe contouring warmth.
- **Cool × medium:** rose-tan, cool caramel, muted berry-brown, or neutral-cool flush.
- **Cool × deep:** cool cocoa, red-brown, wine-brown, or blue-red deep flush used sparingly.
- **Neutral × light:** neutral beige, peach-rose beige, or soft taupe warmth.
- **Neutral × medium:** neutral tan, rose-brown, caramel-beige, or balanced healthy tint.
- **Neutral × deep:** neutral cocoa, deep rosewood-brown, espresso-brown, or balanced red-gold warmth.

### Use carefully
- Shimmery bronzer or obvious contour lines; this variant should read like rested skin.
- Tint lighter than the surrounding skin or beard area; it creates chalk at the jaw.
- Orange bronzer on cool skin or gray contour on warm/deep skin.

### Notes
- Use sheer-to-light coverage only; if it reads as full base makeup, reduce product or switch to skin tonic.
