# Part 1: Frame Rules by Face Shape

## Universal rules (apply to every shape)

- **Eyes must not be hidden.** The pupil sits at or just above the vertical lens center. Thick glossy frames that cut horizontally across the eye line read severe and age the wearer regardless of face shape.
- **Brow line rule.** The top of most frames sits at or just below the natural brow. Two intentional exceptions: **browline/clubmaster** (heavy upper rim mimics the brow) and **cat-eye** (upswept tail rises above the brow).
- **Progressive minimum.** B-measurement (lens height) of **28–30 mm minimum** for standard progressives; 30–34 mm is the comfort sweet spot. Avoid shallow rectangles, half-moon readers, and aggressive wraps for progressives.
- **Frame width.** Total temple-to-temple width should equal or very slightly exceed face temple width. Frames narrower than the temples pinch and widen the face. Optical center must align with PD; oversized frames push the optical center outboard of the pupils and degrade vision, especially in progressives.
- **Bridge fit is independent of face shape.** Low/flat nasal bridges (common across many East/Southeast Asian, Latin American, Indigenous, and some Black faces) need Low Bridge / Asian Fit construction with deeper pads, raised bridge placement, and longer temples.

## Oval

**ID:** `oval`

### Best frame shapes
- Geometric, square, rectangle, wayfarer, browline, aviator, soft cat-eye — almost any silhouette balances; light structure flatters most.

### Use carefully
- Oversized frames that overwhelm the natural symmetry; very narrow shallow rectangles that disrupt proportion.

### Material guidance
- Acetate or metal both work. **Medium thickness is most flattering.** Very chunky acetate flattens the face's natural curve. Titanium lets features lead.

### Bridge style guidance
- Saddle, keyhole, or adjustable pads all work. Keyhole adds vintage character without disrupting balance.

### Brow line guidance
- Standard: top of frame at or just below the natural brow. Cat-eye and browline rises above the brow are also fine since the shape supports both.

### Color/contrast guidance
- Wide latitude. **Warm hair** (auburn, golden brown, warm blonde) → tortoise, honey, amber, gold. **Cool hair** (ash, blue-black, salt-and-pepper) → black, gunmetal, navy, gray crystal.

### Frame width vs. face width guidance
- Match temple-to-temple width exactly. Going wider tips the face long; narrower disrupts symmetry. PD should land at lens optical center.

### Lens height guidance
- **30–35 mm** is ideal and easily fits progressives. Avoid very tall lenses that elongate the face.

---

## Round

**ID:** `round`

### Best frame shapes
- **Rectangle, square, geometric, browline, wayfarer, upswept cat-eye.** Angular silhouettes with clean horizontal top lines add definition and visually elongate.

### Use carefully
- Round, oval, P3, panto, small circular wire frames — they amplify roundness. Avoid undersized or shallow frames; they make the face look fuller.

### Material guidance
- **Thin titanium or metal rectangles** add structure without bulk. Sharp-cornered acetate works. Avoid thick *round* acetate. Wayfarer-style chunky angular acetate is excellent.

### Bridge style guidance
- Keyhole sits frames slightly higher and helps elongate. Saddle is fine. Adjustable pads on metal let you lift the frame to stretch the face vertically.

### Brow line guidance
- Top sits at or just below brow. **Browline frames work especially well** — the strong horizontal upper bar breaks the curve.

### Color/contrast guidance
- **High-contrast dark frames** (black, dark tortoise, deep navy) cut more than light frames. Warm hair + warm tortoise; cool hair + black or gunmetal. Pale, transparent acetates disappear and give no structure.

### Frame width vs. face width guidance
- **Slightly wider than the cheekbones** to elongate. Wider-than-tall lens proportions (e.g., 52×38 over 50×45) flatter most.

### Lens height guidance
- Keep **lens shorter than wide.** Aim for the 28–30 mm progressive minimum, not deeper. Wayfarer and rectangle silhouettes hit this naturally.

---

## Square

**ID:** `square`

### Best frame shapes
- **Round, oval, P3/panto, soft cat-eye, semi-rimless.** Curves balance the angular jaw; the contrast softens it.

### Use carefully
- Sharp rectangles, hard squares, heavy geometric shapes, thick boxy browlines — they mirror and amplify the jaw. Wayfarers can work only in soft-corner versions.

### Material guidance
- **Thin titanium or wire metal** is the textbook choice — it softens without competing with the jawline. Thick black acetate squares compete and read heavy. If using acetate, choose **rounded panto in light or translucent colors.**

### Bridge style guidance
- Adjustable pads on metal lift frames slightly higher on the nose, adding vertical length. Saddle on acetate panto also works. Keyhole pairs well with round acetate.

### Brow line guidance
- Standard placement at or just below brow. Soft cat-eye rising above the brow tail is excellent here.

### Color/contrast guidance
- **Lighter mid-tone neutrals** (rose-water, crystal, light tortoise, champagne, brushed silver) reduce visual weight on an already strong face. Avoid stark glossy black thick acetate. Warm undertone → blonde tortoise, gold; cool undertone → silver, gunmetal, dusty blue.

### Frame width vs. face width guidance
- **Slightly wider than the widest cheek.** Frames narrower than the face make the jaw appear wider by contrast. Sit them slightly elevated on the nose.

### Lens height guidance
- Slightly taller (**30–34 mm**) helps elongate vertically and easily fits progressives. Round and oval shapes give natural depth.

---

## Rectangle

**ID:** `rectangle`

### Best frame shapes
- **Oversized rounds, P3, soft squares with deep lenses, wayfarer with depth, oval, browline.** Want depth and curves to break vertical length and soften angles.

### Use carefully
- Small or narrow rectangles (extend length); shallow lenses; thin wire frames that disappear and emphasize length.

### Material guidance
- **Thicker acetate** adds visual weight that breaks up length. Tortoise and two-tone acetate work well. Bold browline (acetate top, metal bottom) is a signature pick.

### Bridge style guidance
- Saddle or keyhole on acetate keep the frame planted lower on the bridge and visually shorten the face. Avoid frames that ride very high.

### Brow line guidance
- Top of frame at brow. A browline with a strong upper bar at the brow horizontally divides and shortens the face.

### Color/contrast guidance
- **Higher-contrast frames** (dark tortoise, black acetate, two-tone) add mass and shorten. Warm hair → warm tortoise; cool hair → black or deep navy.

### Frame width vs. face width guidance
- Match temple-to-temple width. **Do not undersize** — small frames dramatically lengthen a rectangle face.

### Lens height guidance
- **Deeper lenses (33–38 mm).** Taller lens height visually shortens the face. Excellent progressive compatibility.

---

## Oblong

**ID:** `oblong`

### Best frame shapes
- **Oversized round, deep wayfarer, P3, large square with rounded corners, cat-eye, aviator with lens taller than wide.** Anything that adds horizontal weight and width.

### Use carefully
- Small narrow ovals, thin rimless wires, shallow rectangles, tiny circular Lennon styles — all elongate further.

### Material guidance
- **Thicker acetate, decorative or two-tone temples, contrasting upper/lower rims.** Visual interest at the temple adds horizontal width. Thin metal alone usually disappears.

### Bridge style guidance
- Saddle or keyhole keeps the frame mid-nose. A keyhole bridge with detail draws the eye horizontally.

### Brow line guidance
- Sit at brow. A strong upper bar (browline) horizontally cuts the face length and is very flattering.

### Color/contrast guidance
- Bold colors and patterns are encouraged — they add weight where the face needs it. Warm undertone → rich tortoise, cinnamon, burgundy; cool undertone → cobalt, deep purple, smoky crystal.

### Frame width vs. face width guidance
- Match or **slightly exceed** temple width — frame presence is the goal. Decorative temple arms multiply perceived width.

### Lens height guidance
- **Tall (32–38 mm)** to break vertical length. Excellent progressive compatibility.

---

## Heart

**ID:** `heart`

### Best frame shapes
- **Oval, round, teardrop aviator, bottom-heavy D-frames, light rimless, semi-rimless, soft rectangles with rounded corners.** Goal: visual weight at the lens bottom to balance a narrow chin.

### Use carefully
- Heavy browline frames, thick straight-top squares, oversized cat-eyes — they pile mass at the already-wide forehead.

### Material guidance
- **Thin metal, titanium, or light translucent acetate** at the top; rimless or semi-rimless reduces top weight. If using acetate, prefer **bottom-heavier rims or two-tone (light top, darker bottom).**

### Bridge style guidance
- Adjustable pads on metal/titanium let frames sit lower to counterbalance the forehead. Avoid keyhole on heavy dark acetate (drags eye to forehead).

### Brow line guidance
- Sit at or just below brow. **Avoid heavy browline frames specifically** — they emphasize the broadest part of the face.

### Color/contrast guidance
- Lighter, translucent tones at the top; tortoise gradient (light at brow, darker at lens bottom) is ideal. Warm hair → champagne, blonde tortoise, rose gold; cool hair → silver, clear crystal, soft slate.

### Frame width vs. face width guidance
- **Slightly wider than the forehead** but not dramatic — too narrow widens the forehead by contrast; too wide overwhelms the chin.

### Lens height guidance
- Medium-deep (**30–34 mm**) with weight toward the bottom; teardrop aviator naturally provides this. Fits progressives well.

---

## Diamond

**ID:** `diamond`

### Best frame shapes
- **Cat-eye, browline/clubmaster, oval, rimless, soft rectangles.** Width and detail at the upper rim balance a narrow forehead and soften dramatic cheekbones.

### Use carefully
- Narrow ovals (echo the narrow forehead and chin); aggressive geometric squares that fight the cheekbones; bare circular thin wire.

### Material guidance
- **Mixed-material browlines** (acetate top + metal bottom) are signature for diamond. Light metal rimless lifts focus to the eyes. Avoid uniformly heavy dark acetate that overwhelms a small forehead.

### Bridge style guidance
- Adjustable nose pads — diamond faces often have narrower nasal bridges, and pads let you fine-tune. Keyhole acetate works on cat-eye.

### Brow line guidance
- **Browline and cat-eye intentionally rising above the brow are textbook for diamond** — they widen the narrow forehead. Standard frames otherwise sit at the brow.

### Color/contrast guidance
- Two-tone or detail at the top (gold accents, contrast browline) pulls the eye upward. Warm undertone → tortoise + gold; cool undertone → black browline + silver bottom; bold jewel-tone tops work beautifully.

### Frame width vs. face width guidance
- **Match cheekbone width**, with the upper rim matching or slightly exceeding the forehead. Do not go narrower than cheekbones — that spotlights cheekbone width.

### Lens height guidance
- Medium (**28–32 mm**). Cat-eye and browline shapes naturally provide enough depth for progressives — verify the B-measurement.

---

## Triangle

**ID:** `triangle`

### Best frame shapes
- **Cat-eye, browline/clubmaster, aviator with brow bar, oversized squares with detail at top, decorative-top frames, semi-rimless with strong upper rim.** Add visual mass and width to the upper face to balance a wider jaw.

### Use carefully
- Bottom-heavy frames, thin rimless that disappear at the brow, narrow ovals, frames smaller than the jaw width — all leave the jaw dominant.

### Material guidance
- **Acetate top with metal bottom (browline construction)** is the strongest choice. Thicker, decorated, or color-blocked upper rims work; pure thin titanium reads too light.

### Bridge style guidance
- Saddle or keyhole on acetate browlines is fine. Adjustable pads on metal browlines let you sit them slightly higher to maximize the forehead-widening effect.

### Brow line guidance
- Browline frames riding above the brow are explicitly the goal. Cat-eye also rides above the brow tail.

### Color/contrast guidance
- **Top-heavy color contrast.** Dark, bold, or patterned tops draw the eye upward. Warm hair → dark tortoise top + gold detailing; cool hair → black-top clubmaster + silver. Metallic brow bars are excellent.

### Frame width vs. face width guidance
- **Wider than the forehead, approaching cheekbone width** — to broaden the upper face. Do not match jaw width or you over-correct.

### Lens height guidance
- Medium (**28–32 mm**); deeper lenses pull weight back down to the jaw. Cat-eye and browline shapes typically meet progressive minimums.
