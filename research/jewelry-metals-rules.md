# Part 1: Metals by Undertone

## Yellow Gold

**ID:** `gold`
**Family:** warm
**Best for undertones:** warm, warm-neutral, neutral, olive
**Best for depths:** light, medium, medium-deep, deep
**Best for chromas:** soft, clear, bright
**Finish options that work:** polished, brushed, matte, hammered
**Watch out:** Very cool or pale-pink undertones can read mismatched against high-shine yellow gold; soften with brushed finish or use as a smaller accent.
**Notes:** The clearest warm metal; strongest when it repeats in watch case, belt hardware, or jewelry.

## Rose Gold

**ID:** `rose-gold`
**Family:** warm
**Best for undertones:** warm, neutral, warm-neutral, cool-neutral, olive
**Best for depths:** light, medium, medium-deep
**Best for chromas:** soft, clear
**Finish options that work:** polished, brushed, matte, hammered
**Watch out:** On very cool or redness-prone undertones, rosy shine can amplify flush; use a softer finish or mix with a cooler anchor.
**Notes:** A bridge metal: warmer than silver, softer than yellow gold, and useful for mixed-metal stacks.

## Brass / Bronze

**ID:** `brass-bronze`
**Family:** warm
**Best for undertones:** warm, warm-neutral, olive
**Best for depths:** medium, medium-deep, deep
**Best for chromas:** soft, clear
**Finish options that work:** brushed, matte, antiqued, hammered
**Watch out:** Light, cool, or very bright coloring can look dulled by heavy antiqued brass; keep scale small or add cleaner metal nearby.
**Notes:** Best for casual, heritage, bohemian, utility, and textured outfits rather than strict polish.

## Copper

**ID:** `copper`
**Family:** warm
**Best for undertones:** warm, warm-neutral, olive
**Best for depths:** light, medium, medium-deep, deep
**Best for chromas:** soft, clear
**Finish options that work:** polished, brushed, matte, antiqued, hammered
**Watch out:** High-shine copper can skew orange on cool undertones; keep it textured, antiqued, or away from the face when uncertain.
**Notes:** Strong character metal; use as a deliberate accent rather than default hardware.

## Silver

**ID:** `silver`
**Family:** cool
**Best for undertones:** cool, neutral, cool-neutral, olive
**Best for depths:** light, medium, medium-deep, deep
**Best for chromas:** soft, clear, bright
**Finish options that work:** polished, brushed, matte, antiqued, hammered
**Watch out:** Warm undertones can read hard or icy against polished silver; brushed, hammered, or mixed-metal versions soften the contrast.
**Notes:** The most flexible cool metal across everyday, work, and event styling.

## Platinum

**ID:** `platinum`
**Family:** cool
**Best for undertones:** cool, neutral, cool-neutral, olive
**Best for depths:** light, medium, medium-deep, deep
**Best for chromas:** clear, bright
**Finish options that work:** polished, brushed, matte
**Watch out:** Soft chroma can be overwhelmed by mirror-bright platinum; use brushed finish or smaller scale.
**Notes:** Reads refined, dense, and formal; strongest for event, heirloom, and polished work contexts.

## White Gold

**ID:** `white-gold`
**Family:** cool
**Best for undertones:** cool, neutral, cool-neutral, olive
**Best for depths:** light, medium, medium-deep, deep
**Best for chromas:** soft, clear, bright
**Finish options that work:** polished, brushed, matte, hammered
**Watch out:** Very warm undertones can find rhodium-bright white gold stark; mix with rose gold or choose satin finish.
**Notes:** A cleaner cool-metal option when silver feels too casual and platinum too heavy.

## Pewter / Antiqued Silver

**ID:** `pewter`
**Family:** cool
**Best for undertones:** cool, neutral, cool-neutral, olive
**Best for depths:** medium, medium-deep, deep
**Best for chromas:** soft, clear
**Finish options that work:** antiqued, brushed, matte, hammered
**Watch out:** Very light or very bright coloring can look gray beside pewter; keep the line delicate or pair with a cleaner cool metal.
**Notes:** Best when the outfit already has texture, depth, or vintage/heritage cues.

# Part 3: Scale

## Delicate

**ID:** `delicate`
**Best for body scale:** Best for petite to average frames, or anyone wanting quiet refinement.
**Best for face features:** Best for fine, narrow, or softly blended features.
**Item categories typically at this scale:** chain, earrings, ring, bracelet, watch
**Use carefully:** On larger frames or high-contrast outfits, delicate pieces can disappear unless layered or repeated.

## Medium

**ID:** `medium`
**Best for body scale:** Best for average to medium-large frames and the safest default for most users.
**Best for face features:** Best for balanced features or mixed fine-and-strong features.
**Item categories typically at this scale:** chain, earrings, ring, bracelet, watch, brooch
**Use carefully:** Too many medium pieces clustered at face, wrist, and neckline can feel crowded; pick one focus zone.

## Chunky / Statement

**ID:** `chunky`
**Best for body scale:** Best for larger frames or for deliberate oversized contrast on average frames.
**Best for face features:** Best for strong, broad, angular, or high-contrast features.
**Item categories typically at this scale:** chain, earrings, ring, bracelet, watch, brooch
**Use carefully:** Near the face, chunky scale can overpower fine features; move statement weight to wrist, ring, or brooch.

# Part 4: Mixed Metals

## Mixed Metals

### Allowed combinations
- gold + rose-gold: Warm tonal mix; let yellow gold anchor and rose gold soften the transition.
- gold + brass-bronze + copper: Warm-family mix; use shared finish texture and keep one metal dominant.
- silver + platinum + white-gold: Cool-family mix; freely combine when shine level and scale are aligned.
- silver + pewter: Cool tonal mix; pewter adds depth while silver keeps the look clean.
- gold + silver: High-contrast two-tone mix; repeat the secondary metal at least twice or use one two-tone bridge piece.
- rose-gold + silver: Soft bridge mix; rose gold warms silver without making the outfit read fully warm.
- white-gold + gold: Crisp formal mix; best when one appears as fine jewelry and the other as watch or hardware.

### Rules
- Anchor with one dominant metal at roughly 70% of the visible hardware and jewelry.
- Accent with up to one secondary metal; repeat it in at least two places so the mix looks intentional.
- Keep the metal nearest the face aligned with undertone when the outfit needs polish.
- Match finish intensity first: polished with polished, brushed with brushed, antiqued with antiqued.
- Count watch case, bag hardware, belt buckle, eyewear hardware, and shoe hardware as part of the metal mix.

### Use carefully
- copper + platinum: Very warm and very cool; separate by zone or bridge with rose gold or silver.
- brass-bronze + polished silver: Rustic depth against icy shine; align finish or reduce one to a small accent.
- three or more metals near the face: Can read busy; keep scale delicate or use one multitone piece as the bridge.
- equal-weight gold + silver: Can look accidental; make one metal clearly dominant.

### Notes
- Mixed metals work best when they look repeated, not random; one anchor, one accent, one finish story.

# Part 5: Watch Guidance

## Watch — Oval

**ID:** `watch-oval`
**Best case sizes (mm):** 34–40mm
**Best case shapes:** round, tonneau, rectangle, square, cushion
**Best strap materials:** leather, metal-bracelet, nato, nylon
**Use carefully:** Oversized cases above 42mm can overpower balanced proportions unless the whole outfit is intentionally bold.

## Watch — Round

**ID:** `watch-round`
**Best case sizes (mm):** 36–42mm
**Best case shapes:** rectangle, square, tonneau, cushion
**Best strap materials:** leather, metal-bracelet, nato, nylon
**Use carefully:** Very small round cases can echo facial softness; choose structure, visible lugs, or a cleaner rectangular line.

## Watch — Square

**ID:** `watch-square`
**Best case sizes (mm):** 36–40mm
**Best case shapes:** round, tonneau, cushion
**Best strap materials:** leather, metal-bracelet
**Use carefully:** Sharp square cases mirror the jaw and amplify angularity; round or cushion cases soften the line.

**ID:** `watch-rectangle`

**Best case shapes:** round, tonneau, cushion
**Best strap materials:** leather, metal-bracelet, nylon
**Use carefully:** Tall rectangular cases can extend the same long/angular line; if rectangular, keep the profile slim and moderate.
